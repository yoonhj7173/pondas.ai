Watching autonomous agents run can feel like watching a dashboard you don't trust. Our answer was a two-point-hospital-style office sim — warm, legible, calm.

## The two-layer color rule

The world stays low-saturation: muted floors, soft carpets, a calm grid. High-saturation color is reserved for one thing — anything you can click or that needs your attention.

> When everything glows, nothing does. So most of the office is quiet on purpose.

## Status you can read at a glance

Every agent shows its state with a monitor glow and an overhead badge: a cyan glow means working, an amber "!" means it needs your input, a red "×" means it failed. The badges stay the same size no matter how far you zoom, so you never miss the one thing that needs you.

The result: a screen full of agents that's restful to leave running, and impossible to misread when something wants your attention.
